Incident Response Process Steps

1.) Preparation
Preparation is everything that should already be in place before an incident happens. This includes logging, monitoring, communication plans, access to tools, and clear team responsibilities. The goal of this phase is to make sure the team is ready to respond in a structured way instead of reacting randomly.

2.) Identification
Identification is the stage where unusual activity is first noticed and reviewed. This could come from an alert, a user report, suspicious log activity, or unexpected system behavior. The goal is to figure out whether the issue is actually an incident and to understand what is known so far.

3.) Containment
Containment focuses on limiting the damage and keeping the issue from spreading. This may include isolating a system, disabling an account, blocking access, or restricting communication between systems. The main purpose is to control the situation while preserving enough information for investigation.

4.) Eradication
Eradication is the process of removing the root cause of the incident. This could involve deleting malicious files, fixing a misconfiguration, removing persistence, patching a vulnerability, or resetting compromised credentials. The goal is to make sure the issue is no longer active in the environment.

5.) Recovery
Recovery is the stage where systems are returned to normal operation in a safe and controlled way. This may include restoring services, reconnecting systems, validating that the issue is gone, and monitoring closely for any sign that it returns. The goal is to resume normal operations without bringing the problem back.

6.) Lessons Learned
Lessons learned happens after the incident has been handled. This stage focuses on reviewing what happened, what worked, what did not work, and what should be improved going forward. The goal is to strengthen future response efforts and reduce the chance of repeating the same mistakes.
